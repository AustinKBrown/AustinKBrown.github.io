10 up, 428 to go!

 

The Dowels are being finished up. The screw eyes are going in. There is a buzz around the commons with the work starting to come together. Yes, this will be something to see. This will be amazing!

 

I'm pretty excited. I've been saying that a lot lately. But it's true. I'm not usually this excited about something, so I'm just enjoying the experience. We've been screwing in these little brass rings to the top of the dowels all day and we've been tying fishing knots all day and we've been cutting line all day. These little tasks would usually be nothing thrilling, but today I was really enjoying myself.

 

Kirby and Aspen put screws in two buckets of dowels for us today. They are amazing people! Isaac helped tie some knots. So it was a very successful day. The work is all coming together and that giant boom lift is still working hard for us.

 

The sight to see was when that first dowel went in. It was something to see. We had a bit of an audience and as it went down into place, the whole scene was silent. We all watched as it dangled there, swaying back and forth. Smiles spread across the faces of everyone watching. Mark then said proudly, "This is going to look incredible." Mark has been supervising this project. It is nice to get that kind of affirmation, and I certainly agree with him. This is going to look incredible. I was telling Baron as we were leaving, "It's only going to look better as we get closer to the end.

Installing Cables

 

The Golden Ceiling is on its way to being completed. I'm so excited to see it coming together. There is something strange about having such a large piece of art permanently installed in a public place.

 

We rented this really amazing lift for the install, since the one they have at the university is not really all that great for this project. That's Baron Preston up on the lift. He has been an incredible help in getting this all put together. He tells me he's a fisherman, so I put him to work tying knots and cutting cables. Tess has been helping me stain the wooden dowels. There is a lot to still do, but it looks like it's going to happen pretty quick here.

There are a total of 438 wooden dowels that are going to be hanging from the rafters of the food court in the commons at The University of Idaho.

 

Most of our supplies are in, but we are still waiting on the fishing line. The order got canceled due to some confusion earlier last week around the time that I was expecting it to come in, so we are a little behind where I was hoping to be at this point. I've been told that it will all be here today. If that is true we should still be able to get all the work done that we had wanted to with this lift we are renting rather than using the one the University has for part.

 

I'm really looking forward to seeing this all up and ready for the public. Every time someone hears that I'm working on this they say they are looking forward to seeing it complete too. I have enjoyed working on this project from the start even though it has been a huge amount of work. Its thrilling to think that I could make a living at this. I'm grateful to the University of Idaho for giving me this opportunity, to my parents, teachers, and friends for always believing that art was the right career path for me. God is good.

 

In a week and a half I believe this will all be up, but I'm still waiting to find out too, so I'll keep you posted.

​

In the meantime, this is what I'm expecting it to look like when it's done.
