It was a beautiful summer day. When I took the photo of the bee and the leek, at first I thought it was a honey bee. There were many honey bees flying about that day and I did take some photos of them too. However, none so striking as this. The moment of realization that I had not captured "nature at it's best," as I had at first intended, made me rethink the value of the capture. I had captured a yellow jacket. "What a terrible waste of a great photo." I thought to myself as I viewed it more closely on my computer, adjusting my digital negative closer to perfection. I nearly deleted the photo right then and there, but I couldn't do it. The blossom and the bee was too striking. The fact that I was looking at something that made me squirm a little made me stare longer. Months later I found the photo again and set it as my desktop photo on my computer. I forgave the yellow jacket for interrupting my nearly perfect photograph. And now, after several years, I see some value that I did not realize before. Would a rose be as beautiful without the thorns? It certainly wouldn't make as useful a metaphor for life and love. These sentiments grew and built slowly but continuously until I believed completely that it was worthy of being painted. I hoped that painting it I could share my experience.

Sometimes the world is good and sometimes it is bad. There are roses and there are thorns.
