The Golden Ceiling

15x20x40 feet.

 

Why do we look up when we are deep in thought? The Golden Ceiling of the Vandal commons will be an inspiration to the thinkers and philosophers, the mathematicians, scientists, engineers, and artists alike. Sitting in reflection, a student looks up toward the heavens. They have been stuck thinking over the complexities of their studies. The clouds shift. The light changes and pours down through the skylights. A glimmer of sun is caught shining brightly as gold upon the waves. The bits of gold leaf and oil in the wood finish catches the light subtly and conjures ideas of hope and inspiration. The heights of the sky, the visual connection to the ocean, and also to the rolling hills of the vast fields of cultured land make reference to both the sublime and man's constant toil. The thoughts evoked lighten the burden on the mind by reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles. They also remind us that there are great things ahead of us for those who persevere.

 

This proposed work of art was chosen to be installed in the University of Idaho Commons over the food court. It will be going in over this summer. An architecture student (Drew Gilbert) helped with some of the renderings to make sure the proportions were correct.

Life of an Installation

 

A video recorded art installation made from masking tape, accompanied by the empty room where the installation had been and the cube of masking tape that remained after the installation had been taken down.

 

Made from: masking tape, video, eleven hours installation, and one hour tear down.

 

We tend to disregard everything we don’t consider the finished product. The “chaos” of our lives, we tell ourselves it is something we need to avoid or push away. We compartmentalize and simplify and limit down what we do until it fits into what we think others expect. Often in doing so we remember only our end result and not the experiences that lead up to that finish. The “chaos” we are trying to avoid is left behind and forgotten.

That process of reaching the end is life… It is beautiful, complex, and difficult. When we lack the courage to show our own chaos, which makes us truly unique, we disregard our own ability to be creative in favor of a desire for safety.

(Smell and Tell)

Made from: masking tape, paper, canning, jars, smells, and memories
