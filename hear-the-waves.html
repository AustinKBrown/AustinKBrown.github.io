Hear the Waves

The Blossom and the Bee

We were walking on the sky. Sunsets at the coast make me feel so small, but not in a bad way. Being insignificant can be nice. Picking up shells and walking  in the sand as I enjoy the rumbling time drifting in and out of the eternal ocean. I don't know if there is a place where I can feel as at peace as I do at the ocean. The high mountains on a cloudy day sometimes remind me of the ocean, the way the clouds form waves when I stand above them. This time it worked the other way around. I was having a mountain top experience looking out into the misty sea. I could sit on the beach for hours and just listen. Why is it so easy to listen there by the waves? The timeless conversation of a million voices at a low roar that ferociously and endlessly are held at bay by the sands of the shoreline.

 

Some lessons I've learned from the sea...

 

Great things can be held back by many that remain together, however small.

 

Conversation once had is worth revisiting.

 

One voice may not be heard no matter how shrill the scream, but a light in the darkness travels many miles without effort.

 

Listening leads to wisdom, even when we do not understand what we hear.
