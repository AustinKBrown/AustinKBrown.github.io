People of the Palouse

I love playing with art and making exciting things happen. But what makes art even more exciting is when other people start getting involved. Large sculptures like the one I'm proposing in the painting above make it fun and easy for anyone to become a part of what makes the art exciting and unique. It also encourages community interaction. When we make something together it becomes significant to everyone and creates a greater feeling of purpose in our lives. Hundreds of years ago it was common for a whole community to come together to build a cathedral. That isn't really common any more, but by making a place that we can join together for something to work on with other people doesn't have to be that expensive or complicated. It could be as simple as walking around and playing with string.

 

If my work gets chosen there will be scissors for cutting the string and words on the wood posts telling the weavers “pull some string”, “play”, “weave something fun”, “add some color”, and “make it grow.” In this way, the people will be encouraged to participate in a way that they may not experience with most art. As a result, the work will take on life and begin to grow. Put simply, this will be really fun for everyone.

 

This painting is a proposal for the DTour art festival in Wisconsin.
